Design and Construction of Mobile Robot for Disposal of Hazardous Materials

Authors: Arbnor Pajaziti, Ensar Topanica, Endrit Hasani, Rigon Kqiku
Publication Date: March 15, 2025
Conference: 4th International Conference on Intelligence-Based Transformations of Technology and Business Trends (ICITTBT-2024)
Proceedings: Bridging Horizons in Artificial Intelligence, Robotics, Cybersecurity, Smart Cities, and Digital Economy
Publisher: Springer Nature
Pages: 9


This paper presents the design and development of a low-cost mobile robotic prototype intended for the remote handling and disposal of hazardous (non-explosive) materials.

The system demonstrates that effective hazardous-material handling robots can be developed without requiring extremely large investments, making such solutions more accessible for developing regions and research institutions.

The robot integrates:

A robust mobile platform

A 5-DOF robotic arm

GPS positioning

Dual-camera vision system

Dual remote-control operation


Project Objectives

The main goals of the project were:

Develop a low-cost hazardous material handling robot

Enable remote control operation in unsafe environments

Implement a 5-degree-of-freedom robotic arm for pick-and-place tasks

Provide real-time location tracking via GPS

Enable visual terrain monitoring through integrated cameras

Design a platform capable of overcoming road obstacles
